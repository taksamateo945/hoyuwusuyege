Why Is Craig Lowndes Retiring?

In 2017, Craig Lowndes had the most disappointing season since joining Triple Eight Race Engineering. He suffered a winless season. However, he denied taking a retirement and announced he would be racing again in 2018.

With the return of new season in 2018, Craig Lowndes was making up for the last year loss. He won 2018 Tasmania SuperSprint after two years.

However, on July 5, Craig Lowndes announced he would retire as a full-time race car driver at the end of the 2018 season. He concludes this is the right time for him to retire. In the retirement speech he gave, he said,

“Although my current contract with Triple Eight finishes in another 18 months’ time, both Roland and I have come to the decision that this will be my last full-time drive in a Supercar.”

“I’ve always said that Bathurst 2006 was the most emotional and toughest race I’ve ever done in my career but I can say with a heavy heart that this has been the hardest decision that myself and my team have had to make.”

“It’s the right time.”

Further, he emphasized that before retirement, he would want to secure a strong result to leave his mark.

“I want to make sure I finish my last full-time season with some strong results in the bag,”

In October 2018, Craig Lowndes won Bathurst 1000 making it his seventh Bathurst 100 win.

1993 saw him winning the Australian Formula Ford Championship and qualifying him for Formula Ford Festival in England and immediately rose to the charts. In 1994, he won his first Sandown and 1996 saw him winning his first Bathurst 1000.
