Kenny Kunene 'Curses' ANC Leaders in Viral Rant Video: &quot;You Are Not Human&quot;

- The Patriotic Alliance's Kenny Kunene recently went off in a clip in which he curses ANC leaders

- In the clip, Kunene uses a number of swearwords to describe his feelings about the ANC, which he also referred to as "evil"

- Locals have had mixed reactions to the clip with many of them divided about whether the things he was saying were alright to say

Kenny Kunene, the Secretary-General of the Patriotic Alliance, was recently recorded ranting about the ANC and its leaders. In the clip, the ex-convict, businessman and now politician can be heard cursing the ANC leaders "in the name of Jesus".

A visibly worked-up Kunene appears to be upset about the state of some houses that South Africans are living in. "You are not human," he can be heard screaming in the eyebrow-raising online clip.

"Shaaaaapaaaaaaa," Bantu Holomisa captioned the clip.

South Africans were left divided by the clip. While some defended the ruling party and its leaders, others criticised Kunene for the manner in which he addressed his grievances. Read a few of their comments below:

@MothomosoSeth said:

"Don't insult the leaders, insult 10 million voters."

@radebe_jacob said:

"I think I understand Mr Kunene loud and clear, sometimes you don't have to judge a person by the look and what had he done before, etc, listen to the man, what is he saying."

@TheCyclone_ said:

"He ate with the Guptas, he opened a club, he brought slay queens into our world. He is the enabler. We should now trust him."

A few years ago Briefly News also reported that on Kenny Kunene’s website, called Weekly Xposé, he published intimate photos of a young woman whom Cyril Ramaphosa allegedly had an affair with.

However, the woman did not give Kenny Kunene permission to publish pics of her. It has been reported that there has been an ongoing war between Cyril Ramaphosa and Kenny Kunene. Many people have said he allegedly used “dirty tricks” against the ANC Deputy President, Cyril Ramaphosa, to ruin his campaign.

According to Kenny, he published the photos because he wanted to prove to all South Africans that the claims of Cyril Ramaphosa having affairs are true.
