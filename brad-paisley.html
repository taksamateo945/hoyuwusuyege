Brad Paisley - Bio, Age, Net Worth, Salary, Height, Married, Nationality, Body Measurement, Career

Brad Douglas Paisley is a three times Grammy Awards winner. Brad Paisley is an American singer, songwriter, and guitarist. Likewise, he debuted in 1999 with the album “Who Needs Pictures” on Arista Nashville. His eleven studio records were certified gold or higher by the Recording Industry Association of America.

Early life and Childhood

He was born on October 28, 1972, in Glen Dale, West Virginia. He is 48 years old as of 2021. His father, Douglas Edward Paisley, was an employee of the West Virginia Department of Transportation, and his mother Sandra Jean Paisley, was a teacher. His nationality is American and he has a mixed ethnicity (Italian-paternal grandfather, English, German, Scottish).

Brad has stated that his love of country music started from his maternal grandfather when he gave Paisley a guitar and taught him how to play at eight years old. At the age of 13, he wrote his first song and even formed a band named Brad Paisley and C-notes. Paisley eventually secured a regular spot on Jamboree USA, for him, was a road to stardom.

Education

Paisley went to John Marshall High School in Glen Dale, West Virginia. Then he studied two years at West Liberty State College in West Liberty, West Virginia. He attended (full scholarship) Belmont University in Nashville, Tennessee,  music business. Similarly, he received a Bachelor of Business Administration degree from the Mike Curb School of Music Business in 1995.

Career and Professional life

As to mention his career, it started right after he graduated. He signed a songwriting contract with EMI Music Publishing, and he wrote David Kersh’s Another You, as well as David Ball’s Watching My Baby Not Come Back.

In the year, 1999 Brad releases his first solo album Who Needs Pictures followed by hit singles He Didn’t Have to Be chart-topping single, We Danced. The album sold more than 1 million copies (certified as platinum) and brought Paisley to fame.

Similarly,  in 2002 Part II released with a hit single I’m Gonna Miss Her (The Fishing Song). He had released his third album, Mud on the Tires (2003). Celebrity, Mud on tires, Whiskey Lullaby are the top-charting songs. This album was called double platinum.

To mention his next albums released Time Well Wasted, 2005 which consists of songs like She’s Everything, Waitin’ on a Woman, etc. and 5th Gear, 2007, Play, 2008, American Saturday Night, 2009, American Saturday Night, 2011, Wheelhouse, 2013, Moonshine in the Trunk, 2014, Love and War, 2016.
